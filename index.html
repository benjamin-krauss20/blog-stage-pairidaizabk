Based in Ath with three flatmates, I drive to the park every morning. Twenty-five minutes of Walloon countryside between fields and horses — a decompression bubble before diving into the helpdesk tickets.

I took these photos right from the flat. They capture the atmosphere well: calm, green, far from open-plan offices. A striking contrast with the technical days.

Lessons from the internship

  • Handle the paperwork early: student health cover, car insurance abroad, the internship agreement — sort it all out before day one.
  • Truly immerse yourself: make the most of being in a wildlife park. A lunch break among the pandas recharges you as much as a coffee.
  • Network internally: breaks with the IT team are often where you learn the most — the undocumented fixes, the in-house practices.
  • Document as you go: never put off the docs. A ticket closed without a resolution note is technical debt for the whole team.
